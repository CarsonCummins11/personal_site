READ FIRST — DISCLAIMER. All coordinates, tracks, and waypoints here are approximate and derived from public sources (Mountain Project, WCC, WTA, The Mountaineers, SummitPost, guidebooks). Some are explicitly inferred or unconfirmed. Rock, bolts, anchors, and closures change — a basalt column collapsed at Tieton in 2017. Climbing and especially top-rope soloing are dangerous; no one checks your system. Verify every anchor, edge, approach, tide, and closure yourself. Respect raptor closures and land-manager rules. This atlas is informational only.

Investigated but excluded

These were researched and did not meet all three criteria (GPX-grade approach, confirmed foot access to anchors, concrete top-anchor info) and are intentionally omitted rather than guessed:

  • Frenchman Coulee / Vantage — a top-roping destination, but per multiple sources "almost none of the walls have a good place to TR" from above; the Feathers pillars require climbing a ~5.4 route or a loose ~5.5 gully downclimb to reach anchors, and there is loose rock on top. No clean walk-up.
  • Castle Rock (Leavenworth) — 400-ft multipitch formation with a walk-off, not a single-pitch walk-to-top TR-solo venue.
  • Peshastin Pinnacles — mostly slab leads with walk-offs/raps; soft sandstone; top access to set TRs not cleanly documented for most formations.
  • Index Town Walls — top access to anchors is limited and not a genuine walk-up.
  • Deep Creek / Rocks of Sharon / Dishman Hills, Banks Lake, Static Point, Mailbox/Dirty Harry's, Mazama/Methow — insufficient combined documentation of all three criteria.
